Who I Am

Science meets strategy,
with people at the center.

I did not take the straightforward path — and I am glad for it. I started with an integrated dual degree at BITS Pilani, studying Biological Sciences and Chemical Engineering at the same time. I was always pulled in two directions: the rigorous, methodical world of science, and the messy, human world of how things get built and shipped.

That tension became my superpower. I went from researching biosensors and pigment purification in the lab to managing product roadmaps and shipping features to thousands of users. I have worked at a startup, interned at a streaming giant, and now I am back in grad school — this time at the precise intersection of biotech, regulatory science, and business.

What drives me is simple: I want to understand how things work deeply enough to make them work better. Whether that is a genomic search system, a cancer therapy supply chain, or a product that millions of people use — I show up with curiosity, structure, and a lot of follow-through.

Bioinformatics RAG Python AI

SRA-RAG: Genomic Search Engine

A Retrieval-Augmented Generation system for querying the NCBI Sequence Read Archive using natural language. Built over 7 weeks (Jan–Mar 2026), the system lets you ask questions like "What mouse studies used RNA-seq?" and returns semantically relevant genomic datasets. Compared embedding models (MiniLM, BGE-base, MPNet-v2) — BGE-base won with ~89% Precision@3.
